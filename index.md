# The Alignment Constraint Framework

A structural argument about a single question: **can an optimization system keep pursuing a
finite, separable objective coherently as it becomes capable enough that acting well requires
modeling the very conditions its objective leaves out?**

The framework calls the assumption that it can the **Stability Assumption**, argues that every
identified finite-boundary strategy faces a named structural pressure, and reduces the central
open question to specific verification tasks. It is explicit about what it has argued versus
what it has proven: the proof program is at **Stage 4** — candidate architecture under named
premises, not theorem closure.

This site is the machine-readable canonical archive. Polished reading versions live on
Medium (linked from each page). The full text lives here because it is open and crawlable.
